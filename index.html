E-Portfolio Overview

Objectives of the E-Portfolio

1. To showcase learning materials and activities on pre-colonial literary genres.

2. To demonstrate understanding of pre-colonial Philippine literature.

3. To reflect on learning experiences as a group.

Grade Level and Quarter

Grade 11 - 1st Quarter

This e-portfolio covers the pre-colonial literature module for senior high school students, focusing on the diverse literary forms and cultural expressions of pre-colonial Philippines.

Authors Information

II. Picture of Learning Material

This section displays the Telechart visual guide for pre-colonial literary genres.

Pre-Colonial Literary Genres Telechart

A visual guide showing the different literary genres of the pre-colonial period and how they reflect early Filipino culture and beliefs.

About This Learning Material

The Telechart serves as a comprehensive visual guide that categorizes and explains the various literary genres from the pre-colonial period of the Philippines. It helps students understand:

  • The different forms of oral and written literature
  • How each genre reflects cultural values and beliefs
  • The geographical distribution of literary forms
  • Connections between different genres

This visual aid is particularly useful for visual learners and helps in quickly grasping the complex relationships between different pre-colonial literary forms.

V. Reflections

This section contains personal and group reflections on the learning experience.

Learning Reflections

Each group member shares their insights and learning experiences from working on this e-portfolio project.

Maria's Reflection

"Creating this e-portfolio has been an enlightening experience. I was amazed by the richness of pre-colonial literature and how it reflects the wisdom and creativity of our ancestors. Working with my group taught me the value of collaboration and how different perspectives can enrich our understanding of literary texts."

Key learning: Appreciating oral traditions as valid literary forms

Juan's Reflection

"I was particularly fascinated by the epic narratives. Reading about heroes like Lam-ang and discovering how these stories were preserved through oral tradition made me realize the importance of cultural preservation. This project helped me connect more deeply with my heritage."

Key learning: The role of literature in cultural identity formation

Andrea's Reflection

"As the design lead, I enjoyed finding ways to visually represent pre-colonial concepts. The challenge was to create materials that are both educational and engaging. This experience taught me that design can enhance learning and make complex concepts more accessible."

Key learning: Visual design as a tool for effective communication

Group Reflection

"As a group, we learned that pre-colonial literature is not just a subject to study but a living tradition that continues to influence contemporary Philippine culture. The collaborative process of creating this e-portfolio helped us appreciate different strengths and perspectives. We realized that technology can be a powerful tool for preserving and promoting cultural heritage."

Collective insight: Bridging traditional knowledge with modern technology
